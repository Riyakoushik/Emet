# BUSINESS-MODEL.md — Cost Structure
## Emet | Personal App Economics

---

## Overview

Emet is not a commercial product. It has no subscriptions, no public users, and no revenue model. The goal is to keep the app reliable and private at effectively zero monthly cost.

---

## v1 Cost Plan

| Service | Plan | Estimated Use | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| Groq API | Free tier | Personal chat usage | $0 |
| Android Studio | Free | Development | $0 |
| Gradle / Android toolchain | Free | Build pipeline | $0 |
| Local storage | Device storage | Messages, memories, backups | $0 |
| Total |  |  | $0/month |

---

## Cost Philosophy

- Local-first storage keeps infrastructure nearly zero.
- No required cloud sync means fewer hidden costs.
- One-user usage keeps AI costs low.
- The app should stay practical even under heavy personal use.

---

## If Usage Grows

The only meaningful variable cost in v1 is AI inference. If message volume becomes unusually high, Groq usage may eventually move beyond the free tier, but personal use should remain inexpensive compared with running any full backend stack.

---

## Why No Monetization

Emet is personal. Turning grief, memory, or intimacy into a business model would distort the purpose of the product.
